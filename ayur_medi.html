OBESITY

In Ayurveda, obesity is known as Medarog, which is caused by the aggravation of Kapha. Kapha is an Ayurvedic humor which is dense, heavy, slow, sticky, wet and cold in nature. It governs all structure and lubrication in the mind and body apart from controlling weight and formation of all the seven tissues - nutritive fluids, blood, fat, muscles, bones, marrow and reproductive tissues.

HAIR FALL

Ayurvedic philosophy attributes hair fall to the rise of the pitta dosha in the body. This dosha can result in excess heat in your constitution which may disrupt metabolic processes and trigger hair loss. Curb pitta by reducing the intake of alcohol, coffee, tea, smoking, oily, spicy and acidic foods. Enough sleep can also ease pitta dosha by relaxing you. A good night’s sleep is the first step to stress management and promoting hair growth.

ACNE

Acne is a skin condition in which there is excess production of oil from the sebaceous glands leading to the blockage of hair follicles.
It is the most common problem of adolescents, and is therefore known as Yauvanpidika in Ayurveda; yauvan means ‘adolescence’ and pidika means ‘pustules or papules’.
Acne usually affects the face, back and shoulders and other areas.
